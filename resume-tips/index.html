Resume Writing Tips for 2025

Updated June 2025 · 10 min read · Written by the PureFreeCV team

01

Understand How ATS Works

Before a human ever reads your resume, an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) parses it into a structured database. If your formatting confuses the parser, your resume may be ranked last — or discarded — before anyone sees it.

ATS systems extract: your name and contact info, work experience dates and employers, job titles, education, and keywords. They struggle with: multi-column layouts, text inside images or tables, headers and footers, fancy fonts, and non-standard section names.

✅ ATS-Friendly

  • Single-column layout
  • Standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills)
  • Plain text in all fields
  • PDF or DOCX format
  • Standard fonts (Inter, Arial, Georgia)

❌ ATS Killers

  • Two-column layouts
  • Tables for content
  • Text in images or text boxes
  • Headers/footers for contact info
  • Creative section names ("Where I've Been")

02

Choose the Right Resume Format

There are three main resume formats. Choosing the wrong one is one of the most common mistakes job seekers make.

  • Chronological (Reverse): Lists your most recent experience first. Best for candidates with a consistent work history in the same field. This is what most recruiters expect and what ATS handles best.
  • Functional: Groups content by skill rather than timeline. Useful if you have large gaps or are changing careers — but often flagged as suspicious by ATS and recruiters alike. Use sparingly.
  • Combination / Hybrid: Opens with a skills summary, then lists experience chronologically. A good middle ground for career changers with solid experience.

For most people: reverse-chronological is the right choice. It's what recruiters expect, and what ATS parses best.

03

Write Bullet Points That Get Interviews

Most resume bullet points are weak descriptions of job duties. Recruiters want to see achievements — results you drove, problems you solved, impact you made.

The CAR Formula
Action Verb + Context + Quantified Result
❌ Weak: "Responsible for managing social media accounts"

✅ Strong: "Grew LinkedIn following by 140% in 6 months by implementing a data-driven content calendar, increasing organic lead generation by 35%"

Key rules for bullet points:

  • Start every bullet with a strong action verb (Led, Drove, Built, Reduced, Increased, Launched, Managed…)
  • Include at least one number per bullet where possible (percentages, revenue, team size, time saved)
  • Keep bullets to 1–2 lines maximum
  • Write in past tense for previous roles, present tense for your current role
  • Aim for 3–5 bullets per role
04

Keyword Strategy: Match the Job Description

ATS systems score your resume partly by how many keywords from the job description appear in your document. This doesn't mean stuffing keywords — it means mirroring the language the employer uses.

How to find the right keywords:

  1. Copy the job description into a text document
  2. Highlight the skills, tools, and qualifications mentioned more than once
  3. Look at 3–5 similar job postings from other employers and find the common terms
  4. Make sure those terms appear naturally in your resume, in context
📌 Tip: If a job mentions "stakeholder management" and you write "managing stakeholders," ATS may not match them. Use the exact phrase where possible.

Add a dedicated Skills section to ensure important keywords are always present, even if they're covered implicitly in your bullet points.

05

Write a Powerful Professional Summary

The professional summary sits at the top of your resume and is the first thing a human reads (after ATS passes the file). It should do one thing: make the recruiter want to keep reading.

Summary Formula
[Years of experience] [Job title] specialising in [key skill/domain]. [Biggest achievement or credential]. [What you're seeking].
✅ "Results-driven Software Engineer with 6 years building scalable backend systems in Python and Go. Led migration of a monolithic application to microservices, reducing API response time by 60%. Seeking a senior role where I can architect solutions that serve millions of users."

Keep your summary to 3–4 sentences. Avoid vague phrases like "hardworking team player" or "passionate professional" — every candidate says these, and they mean nothing to a recruiter.

06

Length, Layout, and Formatting Rules

  • Length: 1 page for under 5 years' experience; 2 pages for 5–15 years; maximum 2 pages unless you are a senior academic or executive. Never pad to fill space.
  • Font size: 10–12pt for body text. No smaller than 10pt.
  • Margins: 0.5–1 inch (1.25–2.5cm) on all sides.
  • File format: PDF is usually safer for human readers. Some ATS prefer DOCX — check the job posting. PureFreeCV exports both.
  • File name: Use your name and role: JaneSmith-MarketingManager-CV.pdf
  • Contact info: Full name, city (not full address), phone, professional email, LinkedIn URL. Skip date of birth, nationality (unless specifically required), and photo (unless you're in a country where this is standard).
07

10 Common Resume Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌

    Using one resume for every application

    Tailor your resume for each role. Use PureFreeCV's "My CVs" to save a master version and create job-specific versions quickly.

  • ❌

    Writing duties instead of achievements

    "Managed a team" tells the recruiter nothing. "Led a 7-person team to deliver a £2M project 3 weeks early" tells a story.

  • ❌

    Including an unprofessional email address

    Use firstname.lastname@gmail.com or similar. Not your university nickname or a decade-old Hotmail.

  • ❌

    Leaving unexplained employment gaps

    Address gaps briefly in your cover letter or summary (freelance work, caregiving, study, health). Silence invites negative assumptions.

  • ❌

    Lying or exaggerating

    Background checks are standard. Exaggerating responsibilities or titles is grounds for immediate rejection or dismissal.

  • ❌

    Using photos (in most countries)

    In the UK, US, Canada, and Australia, photos invite unconscious bias and add no value. Skip the photo unless you're applying in Germany, France, or another country where it's the norm.

  • ❌

    Including "References available on request"

    This is obvious and takes up valuable space. Omit it entirely.

  • ❌

    Inconsistent formatting

    Mixing date formats (Jan 2023 vs 01/2023), inconsistent bullet styles, and varying font sizes signal carelessness.

  • ❌

    Not proofreading

    A single typo in the first line can end your application. Read it backwards to catch errors. Get a second pair of eyes.

  • ❌

    Making it too long

    Recruiters spend an average of 7 seconds on an initial scan. Ruthlessly cut everything that doesn't add value.
